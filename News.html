1. Day of Departure

  • Arrive at the Church at 6am
  • Eat breakfast before arrival
  • Bring money for lunch & pit stops
  • Pack daypack for travel
  • Medicine (if needed) in original containers
  • Verify and collect medication and forms
  • Bring snacks & two bottles of water
  • Depart ~6:30pm
  • Green schedule books handed out to scouts

2. Medical Forms

  • Due June 23
  • Must be complete, with allergy info and meds listed
  • If incomplete, scout may not attend camp

3. Day of Arrival

  • Wear Class B uniform
  • Bring water bottles

4. Personal Gear

  • Scout is responsible for their gear
  • Bring a lock and label everything
  • Extra shoes, scout book, sunscreen (no aerosol)
  • Supplies for merit badges

5. Spending Money

  • Scout manages their money (suggest $5s/$10s)
  • Parent restrictions optional, SM will call if needed
  • Troop loans available with parent permission

6. Snacks

  • Nonperishables only, in sealed containers
  • No melted foods or snacks in tents

7. Phone Policy

  • Scout is responsible for phone
  • Phones off during class; SM may secure it
  • No shower pics, encourage appropriate photos
  • Scout calls home after dayâ€™s activities (not during)
  • Emergencies: contact SMs

8. Clothing

  • Follow gear list for proper attire
  • No inappropriate shirts
  • Closed-toed shoes required

9. Weather

  • It will be HOT
  • Hydration and sunscreen are a must
  • Weather monitored by SM

10. Homesickness

  • Usually happens at night
  • Scouts encouraged to stay and engage
  • Parents should not suggest coming home

11. Injuries

  • All injuries reported to Scoutmaster
  • Minor: treated onsite; Major: parents called
  • Most common: heat or foot-related

12. Discipline

  • Issues discussed with SM first
  • Serious problems will involve parents
  • Ongoing issues may result in early departure
