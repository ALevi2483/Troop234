What the Scout provides vs what the troop provides

The Scout and family are responsible for:

  • Scout uniform (Class A shirt, pants or shorts, belt)
  • Personal camping gear as Scouts progress
  • Monthly troop dues
  • Participation in activities and events

The troop provides:

  • Program planning and trained adult leadership
  • Advancement guidance and Boards of Review
  • Troop equipment for campouts and activities
  • Leadership development opportunities

Arrow of Light bridgeover Scouts:

  • New shoulder loops
  • Troop bolo tie
  • Scouts BSA Handbook

For Scouts bridging over from Arrow of Light, these items are provided by Troop 234 at no cost to the Scout.

Your first meeting
  • Arrive 10 minutes early if possible
  • Parents stay for the first 10 to 15 minutes
  • Scout will join a patrol or new Scout group
  • Bring a water bottle and notebook
A typical month in Troop 234
  • Weekly Monday meetings
  • One campout or outdoor activity
  • Advancement coaching built into meetings
  • Service projects throughout the year
Troop Organization

Troop 234 is youth-led with adult coaching and support.

Youth leadership: Senior Patrol Leader, Patrol Leaders

Adult support: Scoutmaster, Assistant Scoutmasters, Troop Committee
