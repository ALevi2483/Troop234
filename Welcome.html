How Scouting America Works

Scouting is a youth-led program that builds leadership, outdoor skills, and community service. Cub Scouting is family-oriented. Scouts BSA shifts responsibility to youth, empowering them to plan and lead with adult guidance.

  • Scouts work on advancement at their own pace.
  • Scouts operate in patrols, which are small teams within the troop.
  • Scouts plan and lead activities, campouts, and service projects.
Troop Organization

Troop 234 runs as a youth-led troop. Adult leaders coach and support.

Youth leadership

  • Senior Patrol Leader (SPL) and Assistant Senior Patrol Leader (ASPL)
  • Patrol Leaders (PL) and Assistant Patrol Leaders (APL)
  • Other leadership roles as needed (Instructor, Quartermaster, Chaplain Aide, Webmaster, Historian, Librarian, OA Unit Rep)

Adult support

  • Scoutmaster and Assistant Scoutmasters
  • Troop Committee (Committee Chair, Treasurer, Secretary, Advancement, Fundraising, and more)
  • Chartered Organization Representative

If you want to help, we can match you with a small, clear role. You do not need prior Scouting experience.

Advancement and Ranks

Scouts advance by learning skills, participating, serving, and leading. Most new Scouts focus on Scout through First Class early on.

  • Scout
  • Tenderfoot
  • Second Class
  • First Class
  • Star
  • Life
  • Eagle Scout

Eagle Scout requires merit badges, leadership, service, and an Eagle service project.

Uniforms

Class B: Troop t-shirt with clean pants or shorts.
