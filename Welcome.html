Welcome to Troop 234

New to the troop? Click the sections below to learn more about how we operate.

Troop Structure
Troop 234 is Scout-led, meaning Scouts take the lead in planning and running meetings, activities, and events. The Scoutmaster and Assistant Scoutmasters provide mentorship, while the Troop Committee supports logistics, fundraising, and administration.
Meeting Schedule
We meet every Monday from 7:00–8:30 PM at Covenant United Methodist Church. Please arrive 5–10 minutes early in Class A uniform unless told otherwise.
Scout Expectations
Scouts are expected to uphold the Scout Oath and Law at all times, participate actively, show respect, and take responsibility for advancement and participation. Leadership roles and active engagement help promote rank progression.
Parent Involvement
Parents are encouraged to be active in the troop — whether as committee members, merit badge counselors, or transportation support. A strong partnership between troop leaders and families ensures a quality experience for Scouts.
Camping and Activities
Troop 234 participates in monthly campouts, service projects, and annual summer camp. Permission slips and medical forms must be up-to-date to attend outings.
Uniform and Gear
Class A uniform (shirt, neckerchief, sash) is worn for meetings and formal events. Class B (troop t-shirt) may be used for casual outings. A detailed uniform checklist is available on the Documents page.
Getting Started
New families should complete the BSA Youth Application, submit required forms, and check the Documents & Forms page for onboarding packets. Attend a parent orientation and ask how you can get involved!
